3Managing Teachers

Add teachers and assign them to subjects. Navigate to Sidebar → Teachers.

1
Click + Add Teacher → enter Name, Email, Employee ID → Save.
2
Go to Subjects and assign each subject to a teacher using the Teacher dropdown when creating/editing a subject.
3
Use All Users in the sidebar to reset any teacher's password, change their access state, or disable login without deleting academic history.
Each teacher can see only the subjects assigned to them and can take attendance only for those subjects.
4Parent Accounts

Create parent login accounts and link them to their children. Navigate to Sidebar → Parents.

1
Click + Add Parent → enter the parent's Name and Email → click Create Account. Login credentials are emailed automatically.
2
In the parent's row, click Link Child → select the student and specify the relationship (Father / Mother / Guardian) → Link.
3
One parent can be linked to multiple children. Click Unlink to remove a connection.
4
Parents will immediately see their linked child's attendance, location, fees and exam results on their dashboard.
5
Use Send Class Alerts from the Parents area to manually notify students and parents when a scheduled class has no active session after the timetable slot has passed.
When the student enables location sharing and enters the college campus (within 300 m), the parent receives an automatic email notification.
5Attendance Sessions

Monitor all attendance sessions across subjects from Sidebar → Sessions.

1
The Sessions page lists all sessions with Subject, Teacher, Date, Status and total Present/Absent counts.
2
Click Cancel Session (×) next to an active session to forcefully stop it (use when a teacher forgets to close it).
3
Go to Analytics for department-wise and subject-wise attendance breakdowns with charts.
Attendance can only be marked via face recognition — students must have their face enrolled first.
6Subjects & Departments
1
Departments: Go to Sidebar → Departments → Add Department (name + code). All students and subjects are grouped by department.
2
Subjects: Go to Sidebar → Subjects → Add Subject. Set the Name, Code, Department, Semester and assign a Teacher.
3
Subjects appear automatically for students in the matching Department + Semester combination.
Create all departments and subjects before adding students — students are assigned subjects automatically based on their department and semester.
7Fee Management
1
Go to Sidebar → Fees → click + New Fee Structure → set Title, Academic Year, Amount, and optionally restrict to a Department/Semester.
2
To record a payment, click the Record Payment button next to any student's due amount and enter the amount paid.
3
Students with outstanding balances will see a red fee alert on their dashboard. Parents also see the due amount on their dashboard.
8Exams & Marks
1
Go to Sidebar → Exams → click + Schedule Exam → set Title, Subject, Date, Max Marks.
2
After the exam, the assigned teacher enters marks via their dashboard. Admins can also enter marks from the Exams page.
3
Students can view their marks in My Results. Parents can see results in the child's full report.
4
Use the Marksheet pages to open printable student marksheets generated from stored exam marks.
5
Before printing official marksheets, configure Principal / HoD signatures so the output includes the correct signatories.
9Notice Board
1
Click Sidebar → Notice Board → Post Notice. Enter Title, Content, Category (General / Exam / Holiday / Event / Fee / Urgent) and target audience.
2
Check Pin to top for important notices — pinned notices always appear first with a 📌 icon.
3
Set an Expiry Date for temporary notices — they disappear automatically after the date.
4
Use the category filter buttons (General, Exam, Holiday…) at the top to browse by type.
10Timetable
1
Go to Sidebar → Timetable. Filter by Department and Semester to see the weekly schedule grid.
2
As admin, click Edit Timetable to add, move or remove slots. Each slot needs a Day, Period number, Subject and optional Room.
3
Parents can see today's timetable on their child's detail page including teacher availability status.
11College Settings
1
Go to Sidebar → Settings. Enter your college's official name and address.
2
Set College Location: Type your college name in the search box → the map flies to it and places a red pin. Or click directly on the map. Drag the pin to fine-tune.
3
Upload or update the Principal, HoD and Class Teacher signature images in the same settings area when you need signed printable outputs.
4
Click Save Settings. The college name and location are used on parent maps and arrival notifications.
The college GPS location is the reference point for the student arrival notification — students within 300 m trigger an email to parents.
12Reports & Analytics
1
Go to Sidebar → Analytics for visual charts: overall attendance trend, department-wise breakdown, and subject-wise comparison.
2
Each student has a Download Report (PDF/Excel) button on their profile page with full subject-wise attendance and marks.
3
The student list has an Export CSV button to download all student data including attendance percentages.
4
Use the Marksheet List to filter students by department and semester before opening their official marksheet view.
13ID Cards
1
Open Sidebar → ID Card Template to configure the visual design, principal details, contact info, colors, map location, logo and signature used on every card.
2
Go to Sidebar → ID Cards to review student submissions. Filter by status, department, semester or student name.
3
Use Approve to issue a card and auto-generate its card number, or Reject with a rejection note if the photo or details need correction.
4
Click View to open the full printable digital card with QR code and college map.
14File Manager
1
Open Sidebar → Files to inspect all teaching-content attachments uploaded by teachers.
2
Use the type filter and search box to find files by extension or by the linked content title.
3
Files marked as orphan are present on disk but no longer linked to a content record. Remove them individually or use the orphan clean-up option.
4
Deleting a file also clears the attachment reference from the linked note, assignment, lab or question entry.

2Starting an Attendance Session

Attendance is marked using face recognition. Students must be face-enrolled before this works.

1
Go to Sidebar → Attendance Sessions → click Start New Session.
2
Select the Subject from the dropdown. The date auto-fills to today.
3
Click Start Session. The camera activates. Students walk in front of the camera — the system recognises their face and marks them Present automatically.
4
The session screen shows a live counter of students marked present. Students who are not recognised remain Absent.
5
When all students have been scanned, click Close Session. This finalises the attendance record.
Important: Do not close the browser tab while a session is active. If you must leave, use the Admin panel to cancel the session.
3Managing Past Sessions
1
In Attendance Sessions, click any completed session row to see the full attendance list — who was present, absent or late.
2
You can manually override individual student statuses (e.g., mark someone Present who was misidentified) by clicking their status badge.
3
Use Print Sheet when you need a printable attendance snapshot for a completed session.
4Entering Exam Marks
1

2

3
Fill in all marks and click Save Marks. Students can view their results immediately.
5Leave Requests

2
Click Approve or Reject with an optional comment. Approved leaves are counted separately from regular absences.
6Attendance Reports
1

2
Download either a single-session report or a full subject report depending on whether you need one class or the complete attendance history.
3
Exports are available as Excel (.xlsx) or CSV for sharing with admin or importing into other systems.
Students below 75% attendance are highlighted in red in all reports — share these with students as early warnings.

3
Keep items as Draft while preparing them, then toggle to Published when students should be able to see them.
4
Use Preview to check how notes and supported attachments will appear inside the student app before publishing.
5
Edit or delete old content from the same page when material changes between semesters.

2Face Enrollment

You must complete face enrollment before attendance can be marked for you. Do this once when you first log in.

1
Click the orange Enroll Face button on your dashboard (or go to Sidebar → Face Enrollment).
2
Allow the browser to access your camera when prompted.
3
The system captures 5 photos of your face from slightly different angles automatically. Look directly at the camera and stay still.
4
Once all 5 captures are complete, click Save Enrollment. A green "Face Enrolled ✓" badge replaces the button on your dashboard.
Ensure good lighting and remove glasses/hats for best recognition accuracy. You can re-enroll at any time from the Face Enrollment page.
3Location Sharing

Share your live GPS location with your parents so they can see you are safe and know when you arrive at college.

1
On the dashboard, find the Live Location Sharing card. Click the green Share Location button.
2
An explanation screen appears. Click Allow & Enable Sharing — your browser will ask for GPS permission. Click Allow.
3
The widget changes to show Active 🟢. Your location is now being sent to your parent's map every few seconds.
4
When you arrive within 300 m of the college, your parent automatically receives an email notification.
5
To stop sharing, click Stop Sharing — your location is immediately cleared from the parent's view.
Location data is only visible to parents linked to your account by the admin. It is not stored permanently.

1
Go to Sidebar → Content to browse notes, assignments, labs and question sets published by your teachers.
2
Use the type, subject and search filters to find the right item quickly.
3
Click Read Note or Preview to open the full note inside the app. Supported documents are rendered in-app when possible.
4
Use the Download button when you want the original attachment file on your device.

Approved leaves do not count as absences in attendance calculations — apply as early as possible.

2Live Location Tracking

Track your child's real-time location on an interactive map — similar to delivery tracking.

1
On the dashboard, the mini map updates every 5 seconds when your child has location sharing enabled.
2
Click Full Report → Location tab to open the large interactive map with full controls.
3
The large map shows:
  • Green pulsing dot = your child's live position
  • 🏫 School icon = college location
  • Green trail = recent movement path
4
Use Follow Mode to keep the map centred on your child as they move.
5
Arrival email: When your child enters the college campus (within 300 m), you automatically receive an email with the exact arrival time. This happens once per day.
Location sharing must be enabled by the student from their dashboard. You cannot force-enable it. If the map shows "Location sharing is off", ask your child to enable it.

1

3

Check the timetable's teacher status — if a teacher shows "Unavailable", a class may be cancelled for that period.
